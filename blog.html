Before the complete lock-out

May 5th, 2018

A friend of mine recently suggested I keep up with blog posting for this new 'life-style' change I've been trying. Recently I've become more self-aware of my weight and appearance again, so I decided to go on a salad diet. However, that's not the biggest change in my life-style so far. I've decided to lock myself out of social media (on the exception of Instagram for the purpose of keeping that option open for those that don't have my number). Otherwise, I have sort of etched into it recently before the actual lockout begins. I've deleted all social media apps except IG and have simply been keeping watch of my FB texts through the website with rare replies.

Frankly, I'm hoping this will do well on my mental health. I've always come out online conversations or chats with the fear of being judged and wondering if people see me as annoying or boring. It always felt like one or the other after this experience I've had. And frankly, I wouldn't be surprised if it's true. I think I'm annoying and boring, but I also hope people can see what I can offer.

Off the small rant, I actually think it's done some wonders recently even tho I've been using social media so lightly. I've gotten much more productive the past few days with dance, still waiting on my QA to give me a go-ahead to merge my data into the main develop branch for my side-job, and maintaining myself through jogs and my job. My mood dramatically lightened up in average. I've tried to control my mood before, but it seems like this is more of a real solution than the never-ending attempts I've made. In contrast, I recently replied to a chat today, for some reason, and attempted to clear my facebook chat to control myself further. But I was confronted and I felt myself fall back into this void of fear, of being judged and shamed. Ironically, that's also where I was introduced with the idea of writing a vlog. But I wonder if people will even read this?

I will be posting on this blog for a week with no contact in social media. I may post for longer, I'm not sure. However if this week goes extremely smoothly without social media, I will likely be looking at this life-style change as a concrete one.

Why did this development stop

April 15th, 2018

I'm back, and it's been at least a few months since I did a tiny update on myself. If you've noticed in the projects tab, I actually added and new 'In Progress' project. I've been recruited for a very simple job, to write data for puzzles and perhaps even write functions. Honestly, I was sort of excited going into it because it seemed like a coding task and that I would learn something from this. But all I've been doing is designing puzzles, putting it into the data file or the beta file for testing, then pushing it, and waiting. I'm pretty grateful that Ken gave me a task but I'm also upset with the fact I'm not really getting much out of this but some insight to the code (which I've definitely learned quite a bit through). I had the option to say no, but I didn't because, I guess I expected more.

After this task is done, I need to find my motivation to code again. Whether it is to upgrade and update this site, or to design a game and create it, something has to happen. I'm not progressing with my free time, other than making money off my full-time job in the food industry. That's not how I want to spend this opportunity.

I hope I'll be continuing to update this site, or at least updating this blog to keep tabs and talk about my life so I can reflect on it sometime later.
